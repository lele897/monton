Discover Menton

The Pearl of France & Italy Border

About Menton

Sitting at France's southernmost boundary adjacent to Italy, Menton is where the Maritime Alps meet the Mediterranean (Roche, 2023). Its unique microclimate creates warm winters perfect for citrus cultivation . Stacked pastel terracotta houses, winding cobblestone lanes, and quiet turquoise shorelines form a timeless coastal landscape (French Riviera Tourism Board, 2024).

🚆 How to Get to Menton

Menton is easily accessible from major European cities. Here are the best ways to reach this coastal gem:

✈️ By Air

Fly into Nice Côte d'Azur Airport (NCE), located just 30km away. From the airport, take the LER Express bus or a taxi directly to Menton (approx. 40 mins).

🚄 By Train

The scenic coastal train line connects Menton to Nice, Monaco, and Ventimiglia (Italy). The station is centrally located, just a 5-minute walk from the beach.

🚗 By Car

Take the A8 motorway ("La Provençale") and exit at "Menton". Note that parking can be limited during the Lemon Festival; public lots are recommended.

📍 Top Attractions

Old Town (Vieille Ville)

Wander through narrow cobblestone streets lined with Baroque architecture and discover hidden art studios tucked away in ancient alleyways. The historic heart of Menton offers an authentic glimpse into centuries of Mediterranean life (Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regional Council, 2024).

Basilique Saint-Michel

Menton's iconic landmark featuring a striking 19th-century lemon-yellow façade (Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regional Council, 2024). Climb the famous 99-step staircase flanked by hanging copper lemon wind chimes, and reach the summit for breathtaking panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea.

❤ Like ↗ Share

Musée Jean Cocteau

Housed in a charming seaside castle, this museum showcases the works of French poet and artist Jean Cocteau (Cocteau and Museum Archives, 2022). Explore his film scripts, illustrations, and ceramics in a unique setting that blends art history with stunning ocean views.

❤ Like ↗ Share

🍊 Lemon Festival

Running from mid-February to March, the iconic Lemon Festival celebrates over 130 years of heritage . Over 200 tons of fresh lemons and oranges are hand-carved into towering artistic sculptures following the theme "The Miracle of Life" (Ville de Menton, 2025).
